Common Password Mistakes

  1. Reusing passwords. One breach can compromise many accounts. Use a unique password per site.
  2. Short or simple passwords. Aim for 14+ characters with mixed types, or use a strong passphrase.
  3. Predictable patterns. Avoid keyboard walks (qwerty), dates, names, or sports teams.
  4. Sharing passwords. Use shared vault features instead of sending passwords in chat or email.
  5. Storing in plain text. Don’t keep passwords in notes or spreadsheets without protection.
  6. Ignoring 2FA. Add an extra layer of security to critical accounts.
  7. Not rotating compromised credentials. After a breach, change the password immediately.
